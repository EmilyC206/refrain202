Week 3 - IT 138 - Change management

EmilyC206 April 27, 2026

Have you guys heard of Change Management, or how I learned to love Git & stop hating pull requests? Change management is what makes a single source of truth actually work in programming. Otherwise, how would we actually get work done.

First off, let's define change management. Change management is the study of the process of planning, approving, and deploying changes to services with minimal risk and disruption as explained by Atlasian. The best practices in change management actually stem from a need to reduce incidents as well as meet regulatory standards. The key thing we want to take away is the single source of truth where teams might have been using familiar, legacy documentation; however, it is important to keep documentation up to date.

Essentially, this is where we want a single source of truth where ownership, approval, communication, and training all comes together. Beyond that, I am learning in IT 138 how to deploy that very documentation because of the applicability within many free and/or open source software I depend on in my linux environment.
